Solutions

Built for every part of the listing workflow

From the agent staging a single bedroom to the platform team wiring up a furniture catalogue — CoStage's pipeline fits wherever you sit in the process.

For real estate agents

Stage a listing photo in about 15 seconds

No photographer wait time, no rented furniture. Drop in a photo of an empty room and get a staged preview before your next showing.

  • Drop in a room photo, pick a room type and style, and CoStage stages it in about 15 seconds — right from the homepage demo.
  • CoStage pulls real pieces from your furniture library so the staged room reflects things you can actually source.
  • Not happy with the result? Use "Edit furniture" or "Edit staging" to swap pieces or tweak the look without starting over.
"I used to wait days for a stager. Now I stage the photo myself before I even leave the property." — Independent real estate agent
For listing photographers

Keep furniture consistent across every angle

Stage once, render everywhere. The same furniture pick lands in the same place across every photo from a shoot.

  • The floor plan editor exports placements as world coordinates plus per-camera pixel positions, so a single layout drives every shot.
  • The compositor projects 3D furniture into each room photo at the correct perspective using real camera poses.
  • spatialParser.js turns image-blaster output into a floor polygon, camera poses, and room dimensions automatically.
"The hardest part of staging a full shoot was always making sure the couch didn't move between angles. This handles that for me." — Real estate photographer
For brokers & brokerages

One library, reused across every listing

Build your furniture library once and let your whole team draw from it — no re-sourcing the same sofa for every new listing.

  • The library builder runs any product link through a 4-stage pipeline — clean plate, multi-angle, 3D reconstruction, preview — and saves it to your library.
  • Export costage-library.json as a portable copy your whole team can load and stage from.
  • The catalogue lets anyone on the team browse pieces by source, room, and style before staging.
"Every agent on our team stages from the same library now, so listings have a consistent look without anyone having to think about it." — Brokerage operations lead
For property developers

Visualize units before they're built

Lay out furniture on a floor plan and render it into a photo — useful for pre-construction units where there's no room to photograph yet.

  • The floor plan editor lets you drag furniture onto a 2D plan and export the placements as costage-placements.json.
  • The compositor renders staged photos from those placements using Three.js, projecting GLB furniture into the scene.
  • Reuse the same furniture library across every unit type and floor plan in a development.
"We can show buyers what a unit looks like furnished long before the model unit is ready." — Development sales director
For listing platforms & MLS tools

Integrate the catalogue and pipeline directly

Everything CoStage produces is a plain JSON export or a local API endpoint — easy to wire into an existing platform.

  • backend/server.js exposes a /catalogue?source=... endpoint that returns structured product data via JSON-LD parsing and DOM scraping.
  • costage-library.json and costage-placements.json are portable, versioned exports your platform can ingest directly.
  • No API key required for catalogue scraping — extraction is rule-based and free to run against any supported retailer.
"We pulled the catalogue endpoint straight into our internal tools — no scraping infrastructure of our own to maintain." — Platform integrations engineer
